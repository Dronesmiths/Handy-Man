Preparing Your Home for High Desert Winters

Acton & Leona Valley Edition: Don't let the first freeze catch you off guard.

While Antelope Valley summers get all the headlines, our winters can be surprisingly brutal. For residents in higher elevations like Acton, Leona Valley, and Elizabeth Lake, nighttime temperatures regularly dip well below freezing.

The "Big Three" Winter Risks

Avoiding expensive emergency repairs starts with a few simple preventative measures before the first frost hits.

1. Exposed Plumbing & Irrigation

Backflow preventers and exterior pipes are the first things to burst. We recommend foam insulation sleeves and waterproof tape for any exposed copper or PVC. For Leona Valley properties with wells, ensuring your well-head is insulated is critical.

2. Roof & Gutter Integrity

Heavy desert winds can loosen shingles throughout the year. When winter rains (or occasional snow) arrive, those loose spots become leaks. Clear your gutters of desert dust and debris to prevent ice damming.

3. Weather Stripping & Drafts

The High Desert wind will find every gap in your armor. Replacing worn door sweeps and window seals can save you 20-30% on your heating bill during the winter months.

AV Handyman Pros Winterization Checklist

  • Drain Sprinkler Lines: Turn off the main water to your irrigation and drain the valves.
  • Check Attic Insulation: Ensure your "blanket" is still thick and hasn't been compressed by storage boxes.
  • Service the Furnace: Change filters and ensure the pilot light/ignition is functioning reliably.
  • Inspect Perimeter Fencing: Winter winds can be stronger than summer gusts; ensure your posts are stable.
